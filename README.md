## 📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes over **51,000 retail transactions** from the Global Superstore dataset (2011–2014) to identify the key drivers of sales, profitability, customer behavior, and market performance.

Rather than focusing solely on descriptive reporting, this project combines **Business Intelligence, statistical validation, and customer segmentation** to uncover actionable insights and support data-driven business decisions.

---

## 🚀 Project Highlights

- 📊 Analyzed **51,000+ retail transactions** across four years
- 📈 Developed an interactive **Power BI Dashboard**
- 🧪 Applied **Kruskal–Wallis** and **Dunn's Post-hoc Test** for statistical validation
- 👥 Performed customer segmentation using **K-Means Clustering**
- 💡 Delivered data-driven recommendations for pricing, products, customers, and markets

---

## 🎯 Business Objectives

- Analyze overall business performance.
- Identify key profitability drivers across products.
- Evaluate the impact of discount strategies on profitability.
- Assess market and regional performance.
- Segment customers using K-Means Clustering.
- Develop actionable business recommendations.

# 📈 Key Business Insights

## 📊 Business Performance

- Sales followed a consistent seasonal pattern across four years.
- Profit consistently declined during **July**, indicating a recurring seasonal trend.
- Business performance generally recovered during the second half of the year.

---

## 📦 Product Performance

- Technology generated the highest sales and profit.
- Tables consistently generated negative profit despite relatively high sales, indicating pricing or cost structure issues.
- Furniture generated strong revenue but comparatively lower profitability.

---

## 💰 Pricing Strategy

- Discounts above **20%** significantly reduced profitability.
- Kruskal–Wallis and Dunn's post-hoc tests confirmed that discounts above **20%** did **not** generate statistically significant increases in purchase quantity.
- Heavy discounting reduced margins without producing meaningful sales growth.

---

## 🌍 Market Performance

- APAC generated the highest overall sales and profit.
- Canada achieved strong profit margins while maintaining minimal discount levels.
- Lower-performing markets relied on heavier discounting without proportional profitability improvements.

---

## 👥 Customer Analysis

- Customer segmentation identified **five distinct customer groups** with different purchasing behaviors.
- High-Value customers represented the greatest long-term business value.
- Discount-Dependent customers generated revenue but consistently reduced overall profitability.

---

# 💼 Strategic Business Recommendations

## 💰 Pricing Optimization

- Limit discounts to **20% or below**.
- Reduce excessive discounting on low-margin products.

---

## 📦 Product Optimization

- Prioritize high-margin product categories.
- Review pricing and assortment of underperforming sub-categories.

---

## 👥 Customer Strategy

- Retain High-Value customers through personalized engagement.
- Reactivate At-Risk customers with targeted marketing campaigns.

---

## 🌍 Market Strategy

- Expand growth initiatives in APAC.
- Replicate Canada's low-discount, high-margin pricing approach where appropriate.
